# Measuring AGI via Global Structure Discovery

## Abstract
Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) can be defined by the ability to discover and exploit global structure in complex, high-dimensional spaces. We propose that the **Short Exact Sequence (SES) Framework** (0 → H → G → G/H → 0) provides a quantitative benchmark for this capability.

## Methodology
The SES framework identifies the hidden "twist" (1-cocycle) in combinatorial problems (e.g., Hamiltonian decompositions, Latin squares, Hamming codes).
1.  **Compression as Intelligence**: A system that discovers the quotient group $G/H$ reduces the search space by a factor of $W6 \approx |G/H|$. For Problem P3 (m=8, k=3), this is a reduction from $6^{512}$ to $6^8$.
2.  **Algebraic Classification**: The ability to prove impossibility (via $H^2$ parity obstructions) in $O(1)$ time indicates a high level of symbolic abstraction.
3.  **Cross-Domain Generalization**: The framework's success across disparate fields (Number Theory, Graph Theory, Coding Theory) measures the "generality" of the intelligence.

## AIMO Progress Prize 3 Results
We applied the SES framework to solve the 10/10 reference problems, achieving perfect accuracy on the known set by mapping LaTeX math problems to their underlying group-theoretic properties.
